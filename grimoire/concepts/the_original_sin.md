# The Original Sin

Humans are lazy. 

Your brain turns off when it believes that it is not needed.

This is a feature, but also problematic in the context of using agents as the agents give us a (false) sense of security. 

It's not just a question of trusting agents in spite of their faults. It's a question of optimal usage. If we simply offload
tasks to an agentic stack. Is there really something that we add ourselves? 

In designing grimoire I'm looking to provide tools that **do not make security research easy**. Instead, grimoire's goal is 
to remove friction and provide leverage so you can think harder.
